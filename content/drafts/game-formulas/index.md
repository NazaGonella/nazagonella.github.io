Most popular game franchises have a recognizable formula, the thing that makes these games so addictive and were refined over many entries. Today I would like to analyze the cases of Grand Theft Auto and Persona.

### Grand Theft Auto (5)

Grand Theft Auto is characterized by their open world and freedom to do everything you want, that is, to cause mayhem. Nothing is going to punish you in the long run for commiting crime after crime.

The open world in Grand Theft Auto has shooting and driving mechanics, as well as a sophisticated physics system. You can do whatever you want, with no objectives apart from the ones you impose yourself, so that's why missions exists to contrast that game loop.

During missions, you have a specific objective, the game imposes you a problem you need to solve. These problems are usually skill-based, but nothing sophisticated. For doing these missions, the game rewards you with cool situations, cinematics, and character developments. With sort of like a role-playing feel to it.
